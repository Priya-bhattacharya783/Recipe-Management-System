Madras Chicken Biriyani Recipe


Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 75 minutes

Servings: 4 servings

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice - 2 cups
  • Chicken or Mutton - 500 g (cut into pieces)
  • Onions - 3 large (thinly sliced)
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium (chopped)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste - 2 tbsp
  • Green Chilies - 3 (slit)
  • Curd (Yogurt) - ½ cup
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves - ¼ cup (chopped)
  • Mint Leaves - ¼ cup (chopped)
  • Red Chili Powder - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric Powder - ½ tsp
  • Coriander Powder - 1 tsp
  • Garam Masala or Biryani Masala - 1 tsp
  • Whole Spices - 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1 cinnamon stick, 1 star anise
  • Lemon Juice - 1 tbsp
  • Salt - as required
  • Oil or Ghee - 3 tbsp
  • Water - 4 cups

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and keep aside.
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a large pot. Add whole spices and sauté until aromatic.
  3. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Mix in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Fry until raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  6. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add chicken or mutton pieces and cook until sealed on all sides.
  8. Mix in yogurt, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Cook until the meat is half done.
  9. Add soaked rice and 4 cups of water. Check salt, add garam masala, and lemon juice.
  10. Cover and cook on low flame until rice and meat are fully cooked (dum method can be used for richer flavor).
  11. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork and garnish with fried onions, mint, or coriander.
  12. Serve hot with raita, boiled eggs, or salna (gravy).
